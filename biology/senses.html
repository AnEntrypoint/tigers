Sensory Abilities

Vision

Tigers possess exceptional vision, particularly adapted for low-light conditions. Their eyes contain a reflective layer (tapetum lucidum) that enhances night vision up to 6x that of humans. Vertical slit pupils provide precise depth perception for ambush hunting. Color vision is limited compared to humans.

Hearing

Acute hearing capability spans frequencies up to 60 kHz (humans: 20 kHz). Rotating ear pinnae enable precise sound localization. Critical for detecting prey movements in dense vegetation.

Smell

Well-developed olfactory senses for detecting prey and identifying territorial markers. Scent communication via urine marking conveys reproductive status and territory boundaries.

Touch

Highly sensitive whiskers (vibrissae) around the muzzle and face detect air movement and vibrations. Essential for navigating in darkness and detecting nearby prey or threats.

Taste

Limited taste buds but can distinguish meat quality. Prefer fresh kills with high fat content.
