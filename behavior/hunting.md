# Hunting Techniques

## Ambush Hunting

The primary technique involves silent stalking through cover, then sudden burst from concealment. Tigers rely on camouflage and patience, often waiting hours for ideal opportunity. Close-range pursuit (within 20 m) maximizes success.

## Water Ambush

Tigers may wait partially submerged in water for prey to approach. Powerful build allows taking down prey weighing up to 4x the tiger's weight.

## Prey Selection

Favor large ungulates: sambar deer, chital, barasingha, wild boar, gaur. Also hunt smaller mammals, birds, fish. Select weak or young individuals when available.

## Success Rate

Only 10-20% of hunts succeed. Tigers prefer energy conservation over unsuccessful prolonged pursuits. May kill more than needed when opportunity arises.

## Killing Methods

Deliver fatal bites to throat (large prey causing suffocation) or back of neck severing spinal cord (medium prey).
