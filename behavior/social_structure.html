Social Structure

Solitary Adults

Adult tigers maintain exclusive territories. Brief associations occur only during mating or at large kills. No permanent social bonds between adults.

Mother-Cub Unit

Females raise cubs alone for 2-3 years. Cubs learn hunting, survival skills during this period. Female may allow yearling cubs to share her kill briefly.

Dispersal

Cubs disperse at 2-2.5 years (males earlier). Subadults must establish new territories, often traveling long distances. High mortality during dispersal.

Coalitions

Males may form coalitions with related individuals (brothers, sons). Coalitions can hold better quality territories and increase mating success.
