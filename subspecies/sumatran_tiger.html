Sumatran Tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae)

Description

The smallest living subspecies, endemic to the Indonesian island of Sumatra. Darkest coat of all subspecies.

Physical Features

Compact, agile build. Males weigh 100-140 kg. Dark orange coat with heavy black stripes. Full mane in males.

Population

Approximately 80-150 individuals (2023). Critically endangered. Found only in 3 national parks.

Habitat

Tropical rainforest, peat swamps, montane forest. Dense vegetation essential for survival.

Conservation

Protected areas: Gunung Leuser National Park, Way Kambas, Gunung Simbar. Intense conservation efforts ongoing.
