South China Tiger (Panthera tigris amoyensis)

Description

Also called Amoy tiger. Possibly extinct in the wild — one of the world’s most endangered large predators.

Physical Features

Medium size with distinctive narrow, widely-spaced stripes. Males weigh 130-175 kg.

Population

Possibly extinct in the wild. ~100 individuals remain in Chinese zoos and breeding programs.

Former Habitat

Previously south-central China forests and mountains.

Conservation

Reintroduction programs planned. Origin of all white tigers in captivity.
