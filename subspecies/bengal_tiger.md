# Bengal Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris)

## Description

The Bengal tiger (also called Royal Bengal tiger) is the most numerous surviving subspecies, found primarily in India with populations in Nepal, Bangladesh, and Bhutan.

## Physical Features

Orange coat with relatively narrow, closely-spaced black stripes. Males weigh 180-260 kg; females 100-160 kg.

## Population

Approximately 3,500 individuals (2023). Stable or increasing in well-protected Indian reserves.

## Habitat

Tropical forests, mangroves, savanna. Requires proximity to water.

## Conservation

~50 tiger reserves in India. Ranthambore, Kanha, Bandhavgarh, Sunderbans are key protected areas.
