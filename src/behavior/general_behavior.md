# General Behavior

## Activity Patterns

Tigers are primarily nocturnal and crepuscular, hunting during cooler hours. They rest during midday heat but may forage anytime depending on prey availability and hunger.

## Solitary Nature

Adult tigers are solitary, maintaining exclusive home ranges. Only females with cubs tolerate prolonged association. Males and females come together briefly for mating.

## Territory

Male territories (20-150 km²) typically overlap several female ranges. Scent marking (urine spray, scratch marks) communicates boundaries. Residents recognize individual neighbors.

## Swimming

Excellent swimmers. Tigers regularly bathe to cool down and will swim to hunt or cross water. Unlike domestic cats, they show no hesitation around water.
