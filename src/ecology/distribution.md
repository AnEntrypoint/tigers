# Geographic Distribution

## Historical Range

Once the most widespread big cat: Turkey to Russian Far East, Indonesia to Central Asia. Spanned 12 countries historically.

## Current Range

Highly fragmented across:
- India, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh (Bengal)
- Russia, China (Siberian)
- Indonesia, Sumatra (Sumatran)
- Southeast Asia (indochinese, Malayan)
- China (South China - possibly extinct)

## Range Countries

India, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Russia, China, Thailand, Laos, Vietnam, Cambodia, Malaysia, Indonesia.

## Conservation Priority

Preventing further range contraction. Re-establishing connectivity between isolated populations.
