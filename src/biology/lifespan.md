# Lifespan

Tigers in the wild have an average lifespan of 10 to 15 years, though some subspecies like the Bengal tiger may live closer to 8 to 10 years. In captivity, tigers can live significantly longer, averaging 16 to 20 years, with some individuals reaching 25 to 26 years.

The difference in lifespan between wild and captive tigers highlights the challenges of survival in natural environments compared to the protection and care provided in zoos and sanctuaries.
