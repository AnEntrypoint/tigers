## Subspecies

### Living Subspecies (6)
- **Panthera tigris tigris** — Bengal tiger
- **Panthera tigris altaica** — Siberian/Amur tiger
- **Panthera tigris sumatrae** — Sumatran tiger
- **Panthera tigris corbetti** — Indochinese tiger
- **Panthera tigris jacksoni** — Malayan tiger
- **Panthera tigris amoyensis** — South China tiger (possibly extinct in wild)

### Extinct Subspecies (3)
- **Panthera tigris balica** — Bali tiger (extinct ~1930s)
- **Panthera tigris sondaica** — Javan tiger (extinct ~1980s)
- **Panthera tigris virgata** — Caspian tiger (extinct ~1970s)

## Taxonomic History

The tiger was first described by Carl Linnaeus in 1758 in Systema Naturae. Up to nine subspecies have been proposed, though recent genetic studies suggest only two distinct clades: continental tigers (P. t. tigris) and Sunda tigers (P. t. sondaica).

The IUCN Cat Specialist Group continues to review subspecies classification.
